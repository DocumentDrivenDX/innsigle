Verify

Verification checks crypto and content binding—not truth, purity, or detection scores.

Hierarchy on a verify view

  1. Primary: Signature valid or invalid; issuer id when valid. Or “unsigned declaration.”
  2. Secondary: Composition and ingredient list (mash bill).
  3. Tertiary: Content fingerprint, timestamps, raw claim download.

Dogfood sample

The dogfood page is a signed model-primary artifact. Keys and attestation ship next to it under .well-known/innsigle/. 

node src/cli.mjs verify \
  --attestation docs/dogfood/.well-known/innsigle/claims/index.attestation.json \
  --content docs/dogfood/index.html \
  --keys docs/dogfood/.well-known/innsigle/keys.json

Trust is separate from validity

A valid signature means the key that signed matches the claim. Whether you trust that issuer is policy (TOFU, pin, or later web-of-trust)—same split as SLSA / PGP lessons.

Spec: ADR-001 (Ed25519 + JCS), CONTRACT-001 (CLI exit codes).
