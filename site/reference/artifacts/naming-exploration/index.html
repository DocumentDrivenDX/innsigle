What “mash bill” means

Composition of how the work was made (human, AI, tools, blend)—not a moral grade. Name = the seal. Payload = the mash bill.

Working brand line

Not a verdict. A mash bill for how it was made—sealed with Innsigle.

Name decision: Innsigle

FieldValue
Public brandInnsigle
EtymonOld Norse / Icelandic innsigli — seal, seal-ring; wax on a deed
Spelling choiceInnsigle (anglicized product form). Historical lemma is innsigli (double n, ends in i). Brand spelling favors English word-shape while keeping the seal lineage.
Pronunciation (locked default)INN-siggle — rhymes with single; stress on INN. One word, two syllables after “Inn”: Inn-siggle.
Why this nameTrue document-seal word family; Norse/Azgaard fit; avoids bare Sigli (live AI software company at sigli.com); visual seal carries recognition so the string need not be instantly English
Not chosenSigli (collision), Segl (ON = sail), AI Badge, Mashbill-as-name, English Signet-alone

Name + visual

Operator note: the key element is a visual branding element. Wordmark and spoken name support the seal mark; recognition lives first in the glyph/system, second in the string. Naming doc does not lock mark design—that is design-system / brand work after frame or in parallel discovery.

Guardrails

  • Do not market as AI detection.
  • Do not use “Credentials” (C2PA collision).
  • Do not ship public brand as Sigli alone.
  • Always separate declared mash bill and signed seal from detected fact.
  • Etymology honesty: Latin sigillum line via North Germanic legal vocabulary—not a pre-Christian pure root. Say “Norse/Icelandic for a document seal,” not “ancient pagan word for truth.”
